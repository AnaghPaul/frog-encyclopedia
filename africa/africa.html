Frogs in Africa


West Africa

  1. Goliath Frog (Conraua goliath)

    • Size: 32 cm (12.6 in); up to 3.3 kg (7.3 lbs)
    • Habitat: Fast-flowing rivers in Cameroon & Equatorial Guinea
    • Diet: Insects, crustaceans, small amphibians
    • Unique Feature: The largest frog in the world, known to move rocks for nest-building
    • Conservation Status: Endangered due to habitat destruction and poaching

  2. Hairy Frog (Trichobatrachus robustus)

    • Size: 11 cm (4.3 in)
    • Habitat: Rainforests of Cameroon, Nigeria, and the Congo
    • Diet: Insects, small invertebrates
    • Unique Feature: Breaks its own toe bones to form sharp claws for defense
    • Conservation Status Near Threatened

  3. Lake Oku Clawed Frog (Xenopus longipes)

    • Size: 4 cm (1.6 in)
    • Habitat: Endemic to Lake Oku, Cameroon
    • Diet: Aquatic invertebrates
    • Unique Feature: Fully aquatic, has no tongue
    • Conservation Status Critically Endangered

  4. Ivory Coast Reed Frog (Hyperolius soror)

    • Size: 2-4 cm (0.8-1.6 in)
    • Habitat: Wetlands & forests in Ivory Coast and Ghana
    • Diet: Insects
    • Unique Feature: Changes color from green during the day to brown at night
    • Conservation Status Least Concern
